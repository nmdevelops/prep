Temporary Nomad

I didn't start travelling until I was in my thirties. I didn't feel driven to it. I didn't feel much of anything.

I accidentally discovered that travel was a means of therapy. Of unearthing my broken parts. Of finding replacements. Of becoming whole again.

This story is still being written, and will continue until I die. Like so many others have, I continue to find peace and resolution through connection with human beings, the nature we exist within, and our history of monumenting our moment of existence upon it.

Going to France... My Father, quiet, family and reliance on others.

This travel is what brought me back to life, pulled me out of a pit that I had fallen into. I met a broken, intelligent, narcissist. My Father. A man who I knew little of, and had been curious throughout my life about. He and my stepmother helped me break poor eating habits (as did france generally). I built simple routines over two months of walking 10,000 steps and eating prepared meals. We argued nearly daily. I found some respect for him. I felt he had some for me but little for my experience thus far in life.
